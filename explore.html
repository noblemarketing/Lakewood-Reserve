Raystown Lake Guide

Explore Raystown Lake

Things To Do

Your cabin is only the beginning. Use this local guide to map out lake days, trails, food stops, and low-key moments around Raystown. Everything below is organized to help you plan quickly once you arrive.

When foliage is down—typically late fall through early spring—more lake views open up through the trees. Both The Apex and Anchor Twenty Eight offer water access from the property—you can hike directly down to the water.

Boating & Marinas

Launch points, rentals, and marina access for full days on the water, with Weaver Falls boat launch less than 1 mile away.

Fishing

Popular shoreline spots and deeper water zones for early morning lines.

Hiking & Trails

Scenic overlooks, ridge walks, and easier loops for all pace levels.

State Parks & Nature

Day-use park areas, picnic zones, and nature-first experiences.

Dining & Coffee

Casual local spots for breakfast, post-lake meals, and coffee runs.

Rainy Day Ideas

Backup plans for weather shifts so your itinerary still feels full.
